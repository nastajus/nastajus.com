Hi Ian,

 

New activities:

 

Monocular Flipper Reading (this will build your focusing stamina): with the glasses on

 

Patch one eye.  Using a page in a book, flip the lens in front of the eye (doesn't matter which one you start with).  Make the words clear.  Then read 1 line (words should be made clear before reading).  Then remove the lens.  Make the word clear.  Read across the line.  Then flip to the other lens, etc.  So lens-no lens-other lens, etc.  Do half or full page this way with each eye.

Plus lens: words appear bigger; relaxes focus

Minus Lens: words appear smaller; engages focusing muscles

Feel the difference in your eye muscles between the plus and minus lens. 

 

Brock string (shoelace thickness string, at least 1 meter in length, with 2-3 beads on the string):  glasses on

 

 

Tie one end of string to doorknob, cabinet hardware, secure through a door hinge, etc. 

Hold the string taut and with the other end on the bridge of the nose, directly between the eyes. Put one bead in a random position along the string.  You can try sitting or standing.

Look at the bead carefully. If both eyes are exactly on the bead, you should see what appears to be two strings crossing at the bead, making an " X ".   If you see the strings crossing in front of the bead (over-converging) or further than the bead (not converging enough), this means your eyes are not really pointed AT the bead. Be sure you can get the X at each bead, where the X crosses at the bead.  

If one of the near strings is fading in and out, the eye opposite to that string is trying to suppress.  If a far string is fading, the eye on the same side is tunneling.  

If the far string is only 1 and not 2, it means you are tunneling peripheral vision.  

**The goal for this phase is to get an X at the bead at various locations along the string, looking straight ahead .**  At this point, I am not concerned if the left string is not as solid as the right.

Helpful tips to get the X at the bead if you are having trouble:

Do a few side to side head turns (slowly and small angle) while looking at the bead, and the string still attached to the nose.

Hold your free hand out to the side and snap or wiggle your fingers to remind the brain not to tunnel.  

 

Tap your temple to wake up the eye with the weaker signal (ghosty string opposite to eye).  

 

Touch briefly (pulsing) the bead at arm's length or closer.  

 

Move your body, e.g. shifting weight side to side.

 

Extend your arm out straight to the side to remind your brain about peripheral awareness.
